Meet our teem

John Twesige is a masters graduate in the field of Water resources engineering with policy and governance track from Pan African University Institute of Water and Energy Sciences Including Climate Change (PAUWES) and he has a background in Civil Engineering from Ndejje University (NDU) graduating with second class Upper Honors and currently pursuing a postgraduate diploma in Project planning and Management at Uganda Management Institute (UMI).
He is also a research associate at the College of Engineering, Design, Art and Technology-Makerere University (CEDAT- MAK) and a Wash Facilities Engineer for Infectious Diseases Institute — IDI Knowledge Centre, Makerere University Main Campus. John has participated in The Sanitation and Water for All projects assessment and implementation within Uganda especially the West Nile region supported by CDC and Malteser International.
He has worked on and managed projects focused on sustainable WASH delivery, humanitarian crises as regards WASH, urban development in the context of WASH Priorities and Policies, gender based inclusiveness, governance, and climate change adaptation and mitigation for 5 years and as a consultant to a large number of projects in Uganda, Africa and globally, and has 7 years’ experience in civil engineering projects implementation and evaluation (Construction and Consultancy). His expertise is recognized both in Uganda and internationally by academic and development institutions as a trainer, consultant and researcher on IWRM, Water quality, WASH implementation and planning, humanitarian crises, Water-food systems and security nexus, on-grid and off grid urban aspects of water and energy development, waste water management, Disaster risk vulnerability and management, sanitation and infrastructural development.
John is working on number of yet to be published works in the field of hydrology and water resources, WASH, climate change adaptation and mitigation, infrastructure, humanitarian crises and urban resilience. Therefore, John as an upbeat and educated professional with extensive project management experience, it is a privilege to be applying to your institution. With his current roles as WASH and Facilities Engineer at Infectious Diseases Institute, He is responsible for monitoring interconnected Civil and WASH projects, formulating strategies and defining project objectives. He has also participated in several programs while developing deadlines and budgets that would maximize returns on funds by donors. As an experienced Civil Engineer with more WASH experience, he has deep understanding of project management methods especially in Civil Engineering and WASH sector projects and well-versed in Microsoft Office and project management software and up-to-date WASH technologies.
